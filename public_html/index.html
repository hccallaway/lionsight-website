Modern Battlegrounds

In modern times, large information networks dictate the flow and quality of knowledge consumed by connected users worldwide. From election meddling in the 2016 US elections to the fake news stirring up protests in Indonesia, we have seen the versatile power that information platforms have in disseminating information and mobilising ideas. Mastery in this field is applicable to both public leaders in promoting a narrative and the private sector on the amenability of a people to a product.

Our Story

Our team partnered with the Department of Defense in the Fall of 2019 as part of the Hacking for Defense (h4d.us) program to address the issue of information warfare. Our mission briefing was to find a way to measure the penetration and efficacy of Information Warfare (IW) tactics, and ways to utilise large information networks in IW. Through conversations with DoD beneficiaries, NATO counterparts, professors, and industry professionals, we drilled down to one way that Information Warfare manifested in information networks, in the form of edits on Wikipedia. We have since studied the way edits on Wikipedia have reflected points of contention on the internet and developed a tool to parse and analyze Wikipedia pages for such events. Through the Wikipedia API, our tool currently evaluates Wikipedia pages by the frequency of edits, tone of language used, and relevance to a given topic or list of keywords. These different factors are all taken into consideration in an ultimate page score that may flag whether or not there are malicious edits occuring. Further exploration will include analyzing talk pages, history of identified editors, number of views of inaccurate pages and pages in different languages and testing our product on a large pre-flagged dataset to fine tune our scoring system. We also expect to expand the use of our tool and its algorithm to find spaces of tension in other online platforms.
